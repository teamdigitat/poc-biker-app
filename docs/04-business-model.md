## 2. Revenue Streams (Detail — full pricing in `21-monetization.md`)

1. **Subscriptions** — Free / Premium / Premium+ / Family / Club tiers (recurring, highest-margin stream)
2. **Marketplace commission** — accessories, used bikes, services (5–12% commission)
3. **Ride Pass / Event ticketing** — per-transaction fee on paid events/rallies
4. **Affiliate revenue** — insurance, travel/hotel booking, gear referrals
5. **Advertising** — brand/dealer sponsored posts, banner/native ads, sponsored events
6. **Training & certification** — revenue share with riding schools/academies for paid courses
7. **API/platform licensing** — Phase 3+, white-label and B2B data/API access for OEMs/insurers

## 4. Go-To-Market Strategy

- **City-by-city seeding:** Start in Bengaluru, Pune, Delhi-NCR — highest concentration of premium motorcycle ownership and riding clubs.
- **Club-first acquisition:** Onboard top 500 riding clubs manually with white-glove support before public launch; clubs bring built-in, high-trust user bases.
- **Influencer partnerships:** Recruit riding YouTubers/Instagrammers as founding Creators with early monetization tools.
- **OEM/dealer tie-ins:** Bundle app onboarding with new motorcycle purchases (QR code in owner's manual/dealership).
- **Safety-first PR narrative:** Position crash-detection/SOS as the flagship differentiator for press and word-of-mouth (families recommending app to riders).
- **Motorsport pathway PR:** Partner with MMSC-affiliated academies for credibility in the MotoGP Career module.

## 5. Competitive Moat

1. **Data network effects** — road quality, hazard reports, and route data improve with every rider, hard to replicate.
2. **Safety trust** — once a family trusts the SOS/crash-detection system, switching cost is emotional, not just functional.
3. **Club/community lock-in** — clubs migrating their entire structure (members, events, history) creates high switching cost.
4. **Motorsport pathway credibility** — first-mover structured pipeline partnered with real academies.
5. **Marketplace trust layer** — verified sellers/mechanics with ride-linked reputation, hard for generic marketplaces (OLX) to replicate.

## 7. Key Risks to the Business Model

- Over-reliance on subscription conversion if marketplace GMV growth lags.
- Marketplace fraud/trust issues damaging brand if moderation lags growth.
- OEM partnerships may prefer building in-house connected-vehicle apps (mitigation: position as complementary community layer, not a competing OEM app).
