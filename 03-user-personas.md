# 03 — User Personas

For each persona: demographics, goals, pain points, key features used, primary success metric.

## 1. Casual Rider — "Weekend Warrior Rahul"
- 28, IT professional, Pune. Rides Sunday mornings with 2–3 friends.
- Goals: Discover new short routes, track basic stats, stay safe on unfamiliar roads.
- Pain points: Doesn't know good routes; family worries when he rides alone.
- Key features: Ride planning, live tracking share-with-family, ride stats, feed.
- Success metric: Weekly ride tracked + at least 1 family member added as SOS contact.

## 2. Tourer — "Long-Haul Leela"
- 34, marketing manager, Bengaluru. Does 1–2 multi-day tours per year (Ladakh, Spiti, coastal Karnataka).
- Goals: Multi-day route planning, hotel/camping stops, fuel/EV planning, GPX export.
- Pain points: Planning across multiple apps (Maps, Booking.com, weather, forums).
- Key features: Route Planner with fuel/hotel stops, offline maps, weather warnings, ride replay.
- Success metric: Completes a multi-day tour fully planned inside the app.

## 3. Adventure/Off-Road Rider — "Trail Tanmay"
- 30, works in Delhi, rides KTM 390 Adventure. Off-roads on weekends near Aravallis.
- Goals: Off-road trail discovery, road/terrain quality data, community of ADV riders.
- Pain points: No terrain-specific data on standard maps; risk of getting stranded.
- Key features: Adventure/off-road routes, road quality reports, SOS, crash detection.
- Success metric: Contributes/uses road hazard reports on off-road trails.

## 4. Royal Enfield Rider — "Classic Chetan"
- 40, small business owner, Jaipur. Long-time RE owner, part of a local RE club.
- Goals: Community identity, club events, maintenance tracking for an older bike.
- Pain points: Club coordination via WhatsApp is chaotic; no maintenance history record.
- Key features: Club management, garage/maintenance reminders, events, marketplace (used parts).
- Success metric: Active club membership + logs maintenance every service interval.

## 5. KTM Rider — "Duke Deepak"
- 24, student/young professional, aggressive riding style, corners fast, wants performance data.
- Goals: Ride stats (lean angle proxies, speed, braking), performance-oriented community.
- Pain points: No performance analytics for street riding; wants to see how he compares to peers.
- Key features: Ride stats/leaderboards, challenges, badges, OBD integration (future).
- Success metric: Weekly leaderboard participation.

## 6. Superbike Rider — "Track-Day Tara"
- 32, high income, owns a superbike, does occasional track days.
- Goals: Track day discovery, lap timing, performance community, premium gear marketplace.
- Pain points: Track day info scattered across informal circles; no lap data tool.
- Key features: Track mode (lap timer), track day event discovery, premium marketplace, learning (advanced).
- Success metric: Logs a lap-timed track session.

## 7. Track Rider / Racer — "Grid Girish"
- 21, semi-competitive, races in local/state championships.
- Goals: Structured training, race results tracking, sponsor visibility, licensing info.
- Pain points: No digital record of race results/progress; hard to get sponsor attention.
- Key features: MotoGP Career Roadmap, race results, training modules, creator/sponsor profile.
- Success metric: Race result logged + roadmap milestone achieved.

## 8. Female Rider — "Solo Sanya"
- 26, marketing executive, rides solo and with a women's riding group.
- Goals: Safety-first features, trusted community, women-only groups.
- Pain points: Safety concerns riding alone/at night; harassment risk in mixed groups/marketplace.
- Key features: SOS, live tracking, women-only clubs/verified badges, crash detection, moderation tools.
- Success metric: Uses live-tracking-with-contact on every solo ride.

## 9. College Rider — "Campus Kabir"
- 19, engineering student, Coimbatore. Budget bike, rides with college friends.
- Goals: Social discovery, budget-friendly gear marketplace, campus riding groups.
- Pain points: Limited budget; wants social validation/content sharing (reels/stories).
- Key features: Feed/stories/reels, college-based groups, budget marketplace, gamification/badges.
- Success metric: Posts/engages weekly; joins a college riding group.

## 10. Delivery Rider — "Delivery Dinesh"
- 27, gig worker (Swiggy/Zomato-style), rides 8+ hours/day, safety and utility-first.
- Goals: Basic safety net, fuel-efficient routing, maintenance cost tracking on tight margins.
- Pain points: No safety net if he crashes alone; high fuel/maintenance costs eat into earnings.
- Key features: SOS, crash detection, fuel logs, low-cost maintenance reminders. NOT influenced by social/gamification.
- Success metric: SOS/emergency contact configured; fuel log used weekly.

## 11. Riding Club (Organization) — "Club: Deccan Riders"
- Organization persona: club admin manages 200+ members across a city.
- Goals: Member management, event creation/ticketing, official club identity/verification.
- Pain points: No tool for structured membership, dues, ride sign-ups, or attendance tracking.
- Key features: Club Management, Event/Ticketing, Club Subscription tier, moderation tools.
- Success metric: Monthly event created and filled via app.

## 12. Motorcycle Influencer — "Creator Chirag"
- 29, YouTube/Instagram creator with 100K+ followers, does sponsored rides/reviews.
- Goals: Monetization, brand collaboration tools, distinct creator profile, analytics.
- Pain points: No dedicated platform to formalize brand deals or track ride-content performance.
- Key features: Creator Profile, Creator Earnings, Affiliate Links, Analytics, Verified Creator badge.
- Success metric: At least one brand collaboration facilitated through platform.

## 13. Organizer — "Events: RideOrg India"
- Professional event organizer running paid multi-day tours and rallies.
- Goals: Ticketing, logistics tools, payments, marketing reach.
- Pain points: Relies on external ticketing/payment tools disconnected from rider community.
- Key features: Event Tickets, Payments, Business Portal, Marketing/Ads tools.
- Success metric: Ticketed event sells out via in-app discovery.

## 14. Motorcycle Brand — "Brand Portal: XYZ Moto"
- OEM or accessory brand wanting access to a targeted, verified rider audience.
- Goals: Brand page, sponsored content/ads, dealer locator, new model launch buzz.
- Pain points: Generic social ads aren't rider-specific or intent-qualified.
- Key features: Brand Portal, Ads, Sponsored Events, Dealer/Business listing, Analytics.
- Success metric: Campaign CTR/engagement benchmark met.

## 15. Riding School — "Learn2Ride Academy"
- Offers beginner riding lessons/licensing prep.
- Goals: Course listing, student management, certification issuance.
- Pain points: No digital storefront reaching new riders; manual certificate tracking.
- Key features: Learning Platform (courses/lessons/quiz/certificates), Business Portal.
- Success metric: Course enrollments per month.

## 16. Racing Academy — "Apex Racing Academy"
- Trains competitive racers (Moto3/national circuits).
- Goals: Structured curriculum, student progress tracking, sponsor/scouting visibility.
- Pain points: No digital pathway tool to showcase student progress to sponsors.
- Key features: MotoGP Career module, Training Institutes, Race Results, Mentors/Coach tools.
- Success metric: Student roadmap milestones tracked; sponsor connection made.

## 17. MotoGP Aspirant — "Aspiring Racer Aryan"
- 16 (with parental account), dreams of racing professionally.
- Goals: Understand the actual pathway, costs, licenses, and next steps.
- Pain points: No trustworthy, structured information source in India; family skepticism about costs/safety.
- Key features: MotoGP Career Roadmap (age/country-wise), Academies directory, cost estimator, Scholarships info.
- Success metric: Completes roadmap onboarding + connects with a training institute.
